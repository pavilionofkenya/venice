WANJA KIMANI

(b. 1986, Nairobi, Kenya. Lives and works in Northamptonshire, UK)

Wanja Kimani’s work flows between performance, film, text and textiles. She explores memory through the body and the fluidity within social structures that are designed to care for and protect, but mutate into coercive forces within society. Driven by stories about real and imagined people and places, she places herself within narratives and uses her body to explore ritual, objects and the rural landscape. 

In 2021, she was commissioned by the Women’s Art Collection to respond to their exhibition, ‘Maud Sulter: The Centre of the Frame’ and she created a film and publication, Tongues, which explored fairytales, language and play. In her current body of work, she is experimenting with words and natural dyes from plants. She is a PhD candidate in Fine Art at Chelsea College of Arts, University of the Arts London.

ABOUT WANJA KIMANI’S WORK

Title

Weathering Landscapes

Year

2022

Media

Plant-derived pigments on glass

Dimensions

Dimensions Variable

The series of watercolours are the artist’s reflection on the body as a landscape; an ecological site that inherits, evolves and desires. Cased in found picture frames, the body rests in multiple poses, outstretched and curled inward. The works reflect the season they were produced – spring – a time of regeneration.

Title

Weathering Bodies

Year

2022

Media

Video

Duration

6 minutes 32 seconds

The film begins with a retelling of a children’s story about dinosaurs against the backdrop of a rural landscape where birds – descendants of dinosaurs – are playing in a field.

Through childhood recollection, the film explores themes of evolution, hypervisibility and factors that contribute to the weathering down of the body.
